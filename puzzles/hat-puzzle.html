The Hat Puzzle

The Scenario

Three people are standing in a line, one behind the other. Each person can see the hats of the people in front of them, but not their own hat or the hats behind them.

There are 3 hats: 2 black and 1 white. Each person is wearing one hat.

Starting from the back, each person must guess the color of their own hat.

Person 3
Can see Person 1 and 2
Person 2
Can see Person 1
Person 1
Can't see any hats

Hint

Think about what Person 3 can see and what they can deduce from Person 2's response.

Solution

1. Person 3 can see both Person 1 and 2's hats. If they see two black hats, they know their hat must be white.

2. If Person 3 doesn't answer, Person 2 knows that Person 3 didn't see two black hats. If Person 2 sees a black hat on Person 1, they know their hat must be white.

3. If neither Person 3 nor Person 2 answers, Person 1 knows their hat must be white.
